GDPR Fair Processing Notice

Flok Jobs Ltd

Effective Date: 1 February 2026

Introduction

This Fair Processing Notice explains how Flok Jobs Ltd ("Flok Jobs", "we", "us", "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal data in compliance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, and other applicable data protection laws.

This notice applies to:

  • Employers using our staffing platform
  • Workers (chefs, front-of-house staff, porters, and other hospitality professionals)
  • Contractors and service providers
  • Visitors to our website www.flokjobs.com

Purpose of this Notice:

This notice fulfills our legal obligation to inform you about how we process your personal data and to ensure you understand your rights under UK data protection law.

4. Lawful Basis for Processing

We process your personal data only when we have a lawful basis to do so under UK GDPR. The specific lawful basis depends on the purpose of processing:

Contract (Article 6(1)(b))

We process your data to perform our contract with you and deliver the services you've requested:

  • Creating and managing your account
  • Matching workers with shifts and employers with staff
  • Processing bookings and managing shift fulfillment
  • Facilitating payments and financial transactions
  • Providing customer support
  • Enabling platform communication features

What this means: This processing is necessary for us to provide the service you signed up for. Without this processing, we cannot deliver our core platform functionality.

Legal Obligation (Article 6(1)(c))

We process your data to comply with legal obligations imposed by UK and EU law:

  • HMRC Compliance: Tax reporting, PAYE administration, financial record-keeping (Income Tax Act 2007, Corporation Tax Act 2010)
  • Home Office Requirements: Right-to-work checks and verification (Immigration, Asylum and Nationality Act 2006)
  • Employment Law: Compliance with National Minimum Wage Act 1998, Working Time Regulations 1998, Employment Rights Act 1996
  • Health & Safety: Reporting under Health and Safety at Work Act 1974
  • Anti-Money Laundering: Compliance with Money Laundering Regulations 2017
  • Data Protection: Compliance with UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018

What this means: These are legal requirements we must follow. We have no discretion - the law requires us to process this data.

Legitimate Interests (Article 6(1)(f))

We process your data for our legitimate business interests, provided these interests do not override your rights and freedoms:

Our Legitimate Interests Include:

  • Fraud Prevention & Security: Protecting our platform, users, and business from fraud, abuse, and security threats
  • Platform Improvement: Analyzing usage data to improve our services and user experience
  • Business Operations: Operating our business efficiently, including internal analytics, reporting, and administration
  • Dispute Resolution: Resolving complaints, disputes, and managing customer relationships
  • Quality Assurance: Maintaining service standards and platform integrity
  • Marketing to Existing Customers: Sending relevant service updates and offers to current users (soft opt-in)

Balancing Test: We have carefully assessed that these interests do not override your fundamental rights and freedoms. You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests - see Section 8 for details.

Consent (Article 6(1)(a))

We rely on your explicit consent for:

  • Marketing Communications: Promotional emails, newsletters, and marketing messages (you can opt in during registration or in account settings)
  • Non-Essential Cookies: Analytics cookies, marketing cookies, and third-party tracking (managed via cookie consent banner)
  • Optional Data Processing: Profile photos, additional preferences, and optional features
  • Third-Party Data Sharing: Sharing data with partners for purposes beyond core service delivery (where not required by contract or law)

Your Consent Rights:

  • Consent is freely given - you can use our services without consenting to optional processing
  • You can withdraw consent at any time via account settings or by contacting support@flokjobs.com
  • Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal
  • Withdrawal of consent for essential services may limit platform functionality

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this notice, or as required by law.

Retention Periods by Data Category:

Active Account Data:

  • Duration: While your account remains active
  • Rationale: Necessary for ongoing service provision

Worker Employment Records:

  • Duration: 6 years after last engagement on the platform
  • Rationale: HMRC tax record requirements (6 years), employment law dispute limitation period (6 years from termination)
  • Legal basis: Income Tax Act 2007, Corporation Tax Act 2010, Employment Rights Act 1996

Employer Booking Records:

  • Duration: 6 years after last booking activity
  • Rationale: Contract law limitation period, tax obligations, business record-keeping requirements
  • Legal basis: Limitation Act 1980, tax legislation

Payment & Financial Records:

  • Duration: 7 years minimum
  • Rationale: HMRC statutory requirement for business financial records
  • Legal basis: Taxes Management Act 1970, Companies Act 2006

Right-to-Work Verification Records:

  • Duration: 6 years after employment ends (or as updated by Home Office guidance)
  • Rationale: Home Office compliance, defense against illegal working penalties
  • Legal basis: Immigration, Asylum and Nationality Act 2006, Home Office Code of Practice

Dispute & Complaint Records:

  • Duration: 6 years after dispute resolution or complaint closure
  • Rationale: Legal claims limitation period (6 years for contract and tort claims)
  • Legal basis: Limitation Act 1980

Communication & Message Data:

  • Duration: 2-6 years depending on content
  • Rationale: Transactional messages (2 years), dispute-related messages (6 years)
  • Legal basis: Contract performance, legitimate interests

Marketing Consent & Communications:

  • Duration: Until consent is withdrawn, or 3 years of inactivity
  • Rationale: After 3 years without engagement, we assume consent has lapsed
  • Legal basis: Consent, legitimate interests

Analytics & Cookies:

  • Duration: 12-24 months (see Cookie Policy for specifics)
  • Rationale: Short-term analysis, trend identification
  • Legal basis: Consent, legitimate interests

Security & Fraud Prevention Logs:

  • Duration: 3-6 years
  • Rationale: Security incident investigation, fraud prevention
  • Legal basis: Legitimate interests

What Happens After Retention Periods:

Secure Deletion:

  • Data is permanently deleted using secure deletion methods
  • Multi-stage deletion process ensures data cannot be recovered
  • Deletion is logged and auditable

Anonymization:

  • Where useful for statistical purposes, data may be anonymized rather than deleted
  • Anonymized data cannot identify you and is no longer personal data under UK GDPR
  • Used for trend analysis, platform improvement, and business intelligence

Extended Retention (Exceptions): Data may be retained longer than standard periods if:

  • Required by court order or legal hold
  • Subject to ongoing legal proceedings or investigations
  • Necessary for regulatory inquiries or audits
  • You have specifically requested extended retention

Account Closure & Data Deletion:

When you close your account:

  • Non-essential data (profile photos, preferences) deleted within 30 days
  • Transactional data (bookings, messages) retained for 2 years then deleted
  • Legally required records (tax, right-to-work) retained for statutory periods (6-7 years)
  • You can request a full data export before closure via support@flokjobs.com

8. Your Data Rights

You have the following rights under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. We are committed to facilitating the exercise of these rights.

1. Right to Be Informed

What it means: You have the right to clear, transparent information about how we process your personal data.

How we fulfill this: This Fair Processing Notice, our Privacy Policy, and other transparency documents

Your action: Review this notice and contact us with any questions

2. Right of Access (Subject Access Request - SAR)

What it means: You can request a copy of your personal data and information about how we process it.

What you'll receive:

  • A copy of your personal data in a commonly used electronic format
  • Details about purposes of processing, categories of data, and recipients
  • Information about retention periods and your rights
  • Details of automated decision-making (if applicable)

How to request:

  • Email support@flokjobs.com with "Subject Access Request" in the subject line
  • Provide your full name, email address, and account details
  • We may request ID verification to protect your data

Response time: 30 calendar days (may be extended by 60 days for complex requests with notice)

Cost: Free (except for manifestly unfounded or excessive requests)

3. Right to Rectification

What it means: You can request correction of inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated data.

Examples:

  • Update your address or contact details
  • Correct errors in your employment history
  • Update qualifications or certifications

How to request:

  • Update directly in your account settings (preferred)
  • Email support@flokjobs.com with the specific corrections needed

Response time: 30 calendar days (we'll correct data within this period or explain why we cannot)

What happens next: We will notify third parties (e.g., employers who received inaccurate data) where appropriate

4. Right to Erasure ('Right to be Forgotten')

What it means: You can request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances.

When this applies:

  • Data is no longer necessary for the purposes it was collected
  • You withdraw consent (where consent was the lawful basis)
  • You object to processing (and there are no overriding legitimate grounds)
  • Data was processed unlawfully
  • Deletion is required for legal compliance

When we cannot delete your data:

  • We have a legal obligation to retain it (e.g., HMRC tax records, right-to-work checks)
  • We need it for legal claims or defense
  • It's required for public interest or archiving purposes
  • Retention is necessary for contract performance

How to request:

  • Email support@flokjobs.com with "Data Erasure Request" in the subject line
  • Specify which data you want deleted and the reason

Response time: 30 calendar days

What we'll do:

  • Delete data where we're legally able to
  • Explain which data must be retained and why
  • Provide retention timelines for legally required data

5. Right to Restrict Processing

What it means: You can request that we limit how we use your data while we consider another request or resolve a dispute.

When this applies:

  • You contest the accuracy of data (restriction during verification)
  • Processing is unlawful, but you don't want erasure
  • We no longer need the data, but you need it for legal claims
  • You've objected to processing (restriction while we verify our legitimate grounds)

Effect of restriction:

  • We can store the data but not use it
  • Processing only occurs with your consent or for legal claims/rights
  • We'll inform you before lifting the restriction

How to request: Email support@flokjobs.com with "Restriction Request" and the reason

Response time: 30 calendar days

6. Right to Data Portability

What it means: You can obtain your personal data in a machine-readable format and request we transfer it to another service provider.

What you'll receive:

  • Your data in CSV, JSON, or similar structured format
  • Data provided directly to you or transferred to another controller (where technically feasible)

What's included:

  • Data you provided to us (profile, bookings, preferences)
  • Data generated through your use of the platform (shift history, messages)

Not included:

  • Data we derived or inferred (analytics, internal notes)
  • Data about other individuals

How to request:

  • Email support@flokjobs.com with "Data Portability Request"
  • Specify the data you want and the format (if you have a preference)
  • Specify if you want it sent to another controller (provide details)

Response time: 30 calendar days

Cost: Free

7. Right to Object

What it means: You can object to certain types of processing.

Processing you can object to:

a) Direct Marketing:

  • You can object at any time (we must stop immediately)
  • Use "Unsubscribe" links in emails or contact support@flokjobs.com
  • We'll stop marketing within 48 hours

b) Processing for Legitimate Interests:

  • You can object if you have grounds relating to your particular situation
  • We'll stop processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests
  • Examples: fraud prevention, platform security, service improvement

c) Profiling and Automated Decision-Making:

  • You can object to automated decisions that significantly affect you
  • See Section 9 for details

How to object:

  • Email support@flokjobs.com with "Objection to Processing"
  • Explain your specific situation and why you're objecting

Response time:

  • Marketing: Immediate cessation (within 48 hours)
  • Other processing: 30 calendar days to assess and respond

8. Right to Withdraw Consent

What it means: Where processing is based on consent, you can withdraw it at any time.

What this affects:

  • Marketing communications and newsletters
  • Non-essential cookies
  • Optional data processing and features

How to withdraw:

  • Account settings: Manage communication preferences
  • Email links: Click "Unsubscribe" in marketing emails
  • Cookie banner: Adjust cookie preferences
  • Contact us: Email support@flokjobs.com

Effect:

  • We'll stop the processing activity immediately
  • Previous processing remains lawful
  • Withdrawal doesn't affect service delivery (if consent wasn't required for core services)

9. Automated Decision-Making & Profiling

We use automated systems, including algorithms and artificial intelligence, to process data and make decisions that may affect you.

What Automated Decision-Making We Use:

1. Worker-Shift Matching Algorithm

Purpose: Match workers with suitable shifts based on compatibility factors

How it works:

  • Analyzes worker skills, experience, location, availability, and preferences
  • Compares with employer shift requirements (role, skills needed, location, timing)
  • Generates compatibility scores and suggests best matches
  • Ranks shifts for workers and workers for employers

Data used:

  • Profile information (skills, certifications, experience)
  • Location and geographic preferences
  • Shift history and performance ratings
  • Availability calendar
  • Preferences (shift type, hourly rate expectations)

Impact on you:

  • Determines which shifts you see first
  • Influences whether employers see your profile for specific shifts
  • Affects booking recommendations

2. Fraud Detection & Risk Scoring

Purpose: Identify potentially fraudulent accounts or suspicious activity

How it works:

  • Monitors account creation patterns and login behavior
  • Analyzes booking patterns and cancellation rates
  • Detects unusual payment activity or multiple failed transactions
  • Flags accounts for manual review if risk score exceeds threshold

Data used:

  • IP addresses and device information
  • Login patterns and locations
  • Transaction history
  • Behavioral patterns

Impact on you:

  • May trigger additional identity verification
  • Could temporarily restrict account access pending review
  • May prevent creation of duplicate accounts

3. Performance & Rating Systems

Purpose: Calculate performance scores and reliability ratings

How it works:

  • Aggregates feedback from employers/workers
  • Calculates average ratings, attendance rates, punctuality scores
  • Weighs recent performance more heavily than historical data
  • Adjusts visibility in search results based on performance

Data used:

  • Shift attendance records
  • Employer/worker ratings and reviews
  • Cancellation history
  • Punctuality and conduct records

Impact on you:

  • Affects your profile visibility and search ranking
  • Influences employers' booking decisions
  • May impact access to premium or high-demand shifts

4. Pricing & Fee Optimization

Purpose: Determine platform fees and suggested hourly rates

How it works:

  • Analyzes market data and demand patterns
  • Considers shift characteristics (timing, location, urgency)
  • Adjusts suggested rates based on supply and demand
  • Calculates platform fees based on tier and usage

Data used:

  • Historical booking rates
  • Market demand patterns
  • Shift characteristics
  • User subscription tier

Impact on you:

  • Affects suggested hourly rates for shifts
  • Determines platform fees charged

Your Rights Regarding Automated Decisions:

1. Right to Information: You have the right to know:

  • When automated decision-making is being used
  • The logic behind the automated decision
  • The significance and consequences of the decision
  • How it affects you

2. Right to Human Review: You can request that a human reviews any automated decision that significantly affects you.

What constitutes "significant effect":

  • Decisions that affect your access to shifts or earning opportunities
  • Account restrictions or suspensions based on automated flags
  • Permanent changes to your profile visibility or status

How to request human review:

  • Email: support@flokjobs.com
  • Subject: "Human Review Request - Automated Decision"
