GDPR Fair Processing Notice

Flok Jobs Ltd

Effective Date: 1 February 2026

Introduction

This Fair Processing Notice explains how Flok Jobs Ltd ("Flok Jobs", "we", "us", "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal data in compliance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, and other applicable data protection laws.

1. Who We Are

Data Controller: Flok Jobs Ltd

Flok Jobs Ltd operates as a recruitment and staffing platform connecting employers with hospitality workers across the United Kingdom.

Contact Details

  • Address: 128 City Road, London, United Kingdom, EC1V 2NX
  • Email: support@flokjobs.com
  • Website: www.flokjobs.com
  • Data Protection Officer: support@flokjobs.com
  • Company Registration Number: [Insert if applicable]
  • ICO Registration Number: [Insert when registered]

As the data controller, we determine how and why your personal data is processed and are responsible for ensuring compliance with data protection law.

2. Personal Data We Collect

From Workers

Identity & Contact Information

  • Full name, email address, phone number, residential address
  • Date of birth, gender, nationality

Emergency Contacts

  • Names and contact details of emergency contacts

Right to Work Documentation

  • Proof of right to work in the UK
  • Passport details, national ID information
  • Visa status and work permit information
  • Home Office share codes

Professional Information

  • CV and work history
  • Skills, qualifications, and certifications
  • Professional references
  • Training records and certificates

Financial Information

  • Bank account details
  • Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR)
  • National Insurance number
  • Tax code and PAYE information

Profile & Preferences

  • Profile photos (optional)
  • Availability and preferred shift patterns
  • Geographic preferences
  • Dietary requirements
  • Language skills

Compliance & Performance Records

  • Conduct and disciplinary records
  • Attendance and punctuality
  • Health and safety compliance
  • Food hygiene certification

Shift Activity & Communication Data

  • Shift applications, bookings, and cancellations
  • Performance feedback and ratings
  • Messages and support correspondence

From Employers

  • Business and contact details
  • Company registration and VAT details
  • Insurance and verification documents
  • Booking history and shift requirements
  • Payment and billing information
  • Communications and feedback

Automatically Collected Data

  • IP address and device information
  • Browser and operating system details
  • Usage data and navigation patterns
  • Cookies and tracking technologies

For more information about cookies, please refer to our Cookie Policy.

3. Purposes of Processing

We process personal data for:

  • Platform access and account management
  • Matching workers with shifts
  • Payment processing and invoicing
  • Communication and customer support
  • Legal and regulatory compliance
  • Quality assurance and dispute resolution
  • Analytics, security, and fraud prevention
  • Marketing and personalisation (with consent)

4. Lawful Basis for Processing

  • Contract (Article 6(1)(b))
  • Legal Obligation (Article 6(1)(c))
  • Legitimate Interests (Article 6(1)(f))
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a))

5. Data Sharing & Recipients

We share personal data only where necessary, including with:

  • Workers and employers for platform matching
  • Payment providers (Stripe, GoCardless, PayPal)
  • Government authorities (HMRC, Home Office)
  • Cloud and infrastructure providers (AWS, Google Cloud, Cloudflare)
  • Analytics and customer support providers
  • Legal, accounting, and compliance advisors

6. International Data Transfers

Data is primarily stored in the UK/EEA. Where data is transferred internationally, we apply appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses and adequacy decisions.

7. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary, typically between 2 and 7 years depending on legal and operational requirements.

8. Your Data Rights

  • Right to be informed
  • Right of access
  • Right to rectification
  • Right to erasure
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object
  • Right to withdraw consent

9. Automated Decision-Making & Profiling

We use automated systems for shift matching, fraud prevention, performance scoring, and pricing optimisation. You have the right to request human review of significant automated decisions.

10. Your Right to Complain

If you are unhappy with how your data is handled, you may contact us or complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

11. Updates to This Notice

This notice may be updated periodically. Material changes will be communicated by email or prominent website notice.
