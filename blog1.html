UK Hospitality Skills Gap 2025: How Smart Recruitment is Solving Staff Shortages

The UK hospitality industry is grappling with a critical skills shortage, with over 170,000 vacant positions across restaurants, pubs, hotels and bars in 2025 (Source: UKHospitality, 2025). This recruitment crisis impacts customer service quality, operational efficiency and business growth, making innovative talent acquisition strategies essential.

Understanding the Hospitality Skills Gap

The skills shortage stems from multiple factors:

  • Brexit Impact: Reduced access to European workers, who filled 20% of hospitality roles pre-
    Brexit, has tightened the labor market (ONS,2024).
  • Pandemic Shifts: Many workers left for industries offering better work-life balance, or job
    security during COVID-19 disruptions
  • Changing Expectations: Modern workers prioritize flexibility, career growth and competitive
    benefits

Modern Hospitality Recruitment Solutions

Leading hospitality employers are turning to digital platforms to address these challenges. Flok Jobs specialises in connecting venues with qualified candidates using AI-powered matching algorithms, reducing time-to-hire from weeks to days.

Key strategies include:

  • Digital-First Approach: Platforms like Flok Jobs focus on hospitality-specific roles, understanding unique requirements like shift patterns and skill sets
  • Social Media Recruitment: Venues like The Ivy Collection use Instagram and TikTok to showcase vibrant workplace cultures, attracting talent with authentic content.
  • Employee Referral Programs: Pub chain Greene King offers referral bonuses, resulting in 15% of hires coming from staff recommendations, improving retention (Flok Jobs analysis, 2025).

Attracting Talent in Competitive Markets

To stand out, venues must differentiate their employment proposition:

  • Flexible Working: Offering shift-swapping or part-time roles, as seen at Dishoom, accommodates workers’ lifestyles.
  • Career Development: Training programs, like those at Hilton Hotels, provide certifications and clear progression paths, retaining ambitious talent.
  • Competitive Compensation: Beyond wages, venues like Wetherspoons offer transparent tip policies and health benefits to attract top candidates.

Technology’s Role in Recruitment

Recruitment technology streamlines hiring:

  • Automated Matching: Flok Jobs uses AI to match candidates based on skills, location and availability
  • Video Interviewing: Remote screening saves time, as used by Zizzi for initial candidate assessments.
  • Mobile-First Applications: With 70% of hospitality job seekers using mobile devices (Flok Jobs data, 2025), mobile-optimized applications are critical.

Retention Strategies

Reducing turnover is as crucial as hiring:

  • Comprehensive Onboarding: Structured programs, like those at Nando’s, help new hires integrate quickly.
  • Regular Feedback: Monthly check-ins at Bill’s Restaurants improve employee satisfaction.
  • Recognition Programs: Employee-of-the-month schemes at Premier Inn boost morale.

Diversity and Inclusion

Actively recruiting from underrepresented groups expands talent pools. Flok Jobs enables venues to highlight inclusive policies, such as flexible pronouns in job descriptions or outreach to community groups, attracting diverse candidates. For example, Wahaca’s diversity initiatives have increased applications from minority groups by 25% (Flok Jobs case study, 2025).

Regional Considerations

  • London: High living costs demand competitive salaries or accommodation support.
  • Rural Areas: Limited transport requires flexible shifts or travel assistance.
  • Seasonal Destinations: Coastal venues like Cornwall’s Rick Stein restaurants need seasonal hiring strategies.
  • Northern Cities: Manchester venues highlight career growth to compete with tech sectors.

The Future of Recruitment

Emerging trends include:

  • Skills-Based Hiring: Focusing on transferable skills from retail or customer service.
  • Apprenticeships: Government-backed programs train young talent.
  • Remote Roles: Social media management roles attract flexible workers.

Practical Steps for Venues

  • Write compelling job descriptions highlighting culture and growth.
  • Respond to applications within 48 hours to secure top talent.
  • Use Flok Jobs for industry-specific hiring solutions.

Measuring Success

Track metrics like:

  • Time-to-hire (target: under 10 days)
  • Cost-per-hire
  • Retention rates
  • Candidate satisfaction

Conclusion

The UK hospitality skills shortage demands innovative solutions. By leveraging platforms like
Flok Jobs, embracing flexibility, and investing in employee development, venues can build strong
teams. Visit Flok Jobs today to connect with top hospitality talent and address the 2025 skill gap
effectively
