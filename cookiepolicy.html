2. What Are Cookies?

2.1 Definition

Cookies are small text files (typically 4KB or less) stored on your device (computer, tablet, smartphone) when you visit a website. They contain information that a website can recognise on your next visit, allowing sites to remember your preferences, maintain your login status, and track your behaviour for analytics or advertising purposes.

2.2 Similar Technologies

Beyond cookies, we also use similar tracking technologies:

  • Local Storage: Data stored directly on your device (larger capacity than cookies, persists until manually cleared)
  • Session Storage: Temporary storage cleared when you close your browser
  • Web Beacons (Pixels): Invisible 1x1 pixel images that track when you view a page or email
  • SDKs (Software Development Kits): Code embedded in applications to track user behaviour

6. Cookie Management & Your Choices

6.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our website, you will see a cookie consent banner with the following options:

  • Accept All: Enables all cookies (essential, analytics, marketing, preferences)
  • Reject All: Disables all non-essential cookies. Only strictly necessary cookies remain active.
  • Customise: Allows you to choose which cookie categories to enable or disable:
    • ✓ Essential (always on, cannot be disabled)
    • ☐ Analytics
    • ☐ Marketing & Advertising
    • ☐ Preferences & Functionality

Your choice is stored in the flok_consent cookie for 6 months.

6.2 Updating Your Preferences

You can change your cookie preferences at any time:

  1. Visit our Cookie Settings page – Link available in the footer of every page (click "Manage Cookies")
  2. Clear the consent cookie – Delete the flok_consent cookie from your device (banner will reappear on next visit)
  3. Contact us – Email support@flokjobs.com to request changes

Important: If you reject marketing or analytics cookies, those cookies will be deleted from your device immediately. If you later accept them, new cookies will be set.

6.3 Browser Controls

You can manage cookies directly in your web browser:

Google Chrome:

  • Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Allow, block, or delete cookies per site

Mozilla Firefox:

  • Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Choose to delete cookies on exit or manage per-site permissions

Safari (Apple):

  • Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data
  • Block all cookies or allow cookies from visited sites only

Microsoft Edge:

  • Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Cookies and other site data
  • Manage cookie permissions per site

Note: Blocking cookies may impair platform functionality (e.g., you may be logged out, preferences may not be saved).

6.4 Do Not Track (DNT)

Some browsers allow you to send a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. While we respect DNT signals, we cannot guarantee that third-party providers (Google, Meta) will honour DNT requests. Third-party cookie providers have their own privacy policies governing DNT compliance.

6.5 Disabling Cookies Entirely

If you disable all cookies in your browser, you may experience issues:

  • Login problems: You may be logged out unexpectedly
  • Booking failures: Bookings and preferences may not be saved
  • Performance: Some features may not work correctly
  • Personalisation: The platform will not remember your settings

We recommend allowing essential cookies while managing analytics and marketing cookies to your preference.

7. Cookie Data & Privacy

7.1 What Data Is Collected?

Analytics and marketing cookies may collect:

  • Device information: Browser type, operating system, device type, screen resolution
  • Behaviour data: Pages visited, links clicked, time spent on page, search queries
  • Location data: Approximate location based on IP address (not precise GPS location)
  • Interaction data: Shifts viewed, jobs applied for, messages sent, bookings completed
  • Identifiers: User ID (hashed or anonymised), session ID, advertising ID

7.2 Data Protection & Security

Your Data Is Protected:

  • Analytics data is anonymised – we cannot identify you from analytics data alone
  • Marketing data is encrypted when transmitted to Google and Meta
  • All third-party processors are contractually bound to protect your data
  • Flok Jobs does not sell your data to third parties

Consent Requirements:

  • We only set non-essential cookies after you consent
  • You can withdraw consent at any time
  • Withdrawal does not affect prior lawful processing

8. Your GDPR Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding cookie data:

8.1 Right of Access

You can request a copy of all personal data collected via cookies. Contact: support@flokjobs.com with subject "Cookie Data Access Request"

8.2 Right to Erasure

You can request deletion of cookie-derived data (subject to legal obligations). Cookies are typically automatically deleted after their retention period expires.

8.3 Right to Object

You can object to processing via cookies for marketing or analytics purposes. We will stop setting those cookies.

8.4 Right to Withdraw Consent

You can withdraw consent for non-essential cookies at any time by:

  • Using our Cookie Settings tool
  • Adjusting your browser settings
  • Contacting support@flokjobs.com

Withdrawal does not affect lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
