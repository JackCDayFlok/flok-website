Privacy Policy

Flok Jobs Ltd

Effective Date: 1 February 2026

Your Privacy at a Glance

Flok Jobs Ltd ("Flok Jobs", "we", "us", "our") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal data.

Key Points:

  • We collect only the data necessary to run our hospitality staffing platform
  • We never sell your personal data to third parties
  • You have full control over your data and can request access, correction, or deletion at any time
  • We use industry-standard security to protect your information
  • We comply with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018

This Privacy Policy explains in detail how we collect, use, store, and protect your personal data when you use our website www.flokjobs.com (the "Website"), our platform, or our services.

This Privacy Policy applies to:

  • Employers using Flok Jobs to book staff
  • Workers (chefs, FOH, porters, and other hospitality professionals) using Flok Jobs to find shifts
  • Visitors to our Website

1. Data We Collect

We collect and process the following categories of personal data:

For Workers:

  • Personal Details: Full name, contact information (email, phone, address), date of birth, gender
  • Emergency Contacts: Names and contact details of emergency contacts
  • Identification & Right to Work: Proof of right to work documentation, passport/national ID details, right to work verification records
  • Employment Details: CV, employment history, skills, qualifications, certifications, professional references
  • Payment Details: Bank account information, sort code, UTR (Unique Taxpayer Reference), National Insurance number
  • Compliance Records: Uniform compliance records, conduct records, shift attendance and performance records, training completion records
  • Profile Information: Profile photos (if uploaded), work preferences, shift availability
  • Communication Data: Messages, booking confirmations, support correspondence

For Employers:

  • Business Contact Information: Full name, company name, business address, phone, email, website
  • Business Verification: Company registration details, VAT number, business structure
  • Payment Details: Bank account information, payment method for platform fees
  • Booking & Engagement Records: Booking history, worker engagement records, shift preferences, job descriptions, role requirements
  • Communication Data: Messages with workers, support correspondence, feedback

For Website Visitors:

  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type, operating system, device type, internet service provider
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, time spent on pages, links clicked, login activity, search queries
  • Cookies & Analytics: First-party and third-party cookies, pixel tags, and similar tracking technologies (see Section 8 for detailed information)

2. How We Use Your Data

We use your data for the following purposes:

  • Service Delivery: Facilitate worker and employer matching via the Flok Jobs platform
  • Booking & Payments: Process bookings, cancellations, and payments securely
  • Compliance & Verification: Verify worker eligibility, conduct right-to-work checks, and ensure compliance with UK employment law, HMRC requirements, and Home Office regulations
  • Conduct Management: Monitor conduct, uniform compliance, and quality standards to maintain service integrity
  • Dispute Resolution: Manage booking disputes, cancellations, and customer complaints
  • Customer Support: Provide customer support via email, chat, or phone
  • Service Improvement: Improve our website, services, and user experience through analytics and feedback
  • Communications: Send important updates about your account, shifts, legal obligations, and service changes
  • Legal Compliance: Comply with legal, tax, and regulatory requirements including HMRC obligations and employment law
  • Fraud Prevention: Detect, prevent, and address fraud, security, and technical issues
  • Marketing: Send promotional content and marketing communications (only with your explicit consent via opt-in)

We will never sell your personal data to third parties.

3. Lawful Basis for Processing

Under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, we process your data based on:

  • Contract: To provide the services you signed up for (matching, payments, booking confirmations, shift management)
  • Legal Obligation: To comply with UK law including:
    • HMRC tax reporting requirements
    • Home Office right-to-work checks
    • Employment law requirements
    • Health and safety regulations
    • Anti-money laundering regulations
  • Legitimate Interests: For service improvements, fraud prevention, security, and business operations. Our legitimate interests include:
    • Preventing fraud and maintaining platform security
    • Improving our services based on usage patterns
    • Conducting internal business analytics
    • Managing our business operations efficiently
    • Detecting and preventing misuse of our platform

    We only rely on legitimate interests where your rights and freedoms do not override our interests.

  • Consent: Where you have explicitly opted in to:
    • Marketing communications (promotional emails, SMS, or notifications)
    • Non-essential cookies
    • Other optional processing activities

You can withdraw your consent at any time by contacting us or using the unsubscribe link in our emails.

4. Data Sharing

We share your data only when necessary to deliver our services or comply with legal obligations.

Who We Share With:

  • Employers (if you are a Worker): To allow them to engage you for work, review your profile, and manage shifts
  • Workers (if you are an Employer): To allow them to fulfil your bookings and communicate about shifts
  • Payment Processors: Service providers such as Stripe or GoCardless for secure payment processing and financial reconciliation
  • HMRC & Tax Authorities: For tax reporting, right-to-work verification, and compliance with financial regulations
  • Home Office: For right-to-work checks and immigration compliance verification
  • IT & Hosting Providers: Cloud infrastructure providers (e.g., AWS, Microsoft Azure), website hosting providers, and database management services located in the UK/EEA
  • Analytics Providers: Google Analytics (which may transfer data to the US under appropriate safeguards) for understanding user behaviour and service improvement
  • Legal & Compliance Partners: Our legal advisors, compliance consultants, and regulatory consultants
  • Service Providers: Other third-party service providers who assist us in delivering our platform and services
  • Regulators & Authorities: Where required by law, regulators, law enforcement agencies, or courts may require us to disclose your data

Data Sharing Safeguards:

Third parties who process data on our behalf operate under strict obligations:

  • Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) that comply with UK GDPR
  • Appropriate technical and organisational security measures
  • Restrictions on data use - they can only use your data for the specific purposes we've authorized
  • Confidentiality obligations - they must respect your privacy and data protection rights
  • Prohibition on unauthorized disclosure - they cannot share your data with other parties without our authorization

We carefully vet all service providers before engaging them and regularly review their security and compliance practices.

5. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes outlined in this policy, and in accordance with UK GDPR principles and applicable law:

  • Active Account Data: Retained while your account is active and you continue using our services
  • Worker Records: Retained for 6 years after your last engagement with the platform (for tax and legal purposes)
  • Employer Records: Retained for 6 years after your last booking activity (for legal and contractual purposes)
  • Payment & Financial Records: Retained for at least 7 years in line with HMRC requirements and tax law obligations
  • Right-to-Work Verification Records: Retained in accordance with Home Office requirements (typically 2 years after the employment relationship ends, or for the duration of any ongoing legal obligations)
  • Dispute & Complaint Records: Retained for 6 years to defend potential legal claims
  • Marketing Data: Retained until you withdraw consent or we determine it's no longer necessary
  • Cookies & Analytics Data: Retained in accordance with our Cookie Policy (typically 12-24 months)

Retention Justification:

These periods are necessary to:

  • Comply with tax and financial regulations (HMRC requires 6-7 years for business records)
  • Defend against potential employment disputes or legal claims
  • Verify compliance with right-to-work regulations
  • Maintain audit trails for security and fraud detection
  • Support ongoing platform operations and service improvement

Secure Deletion:

After the retention period expires, we will:

  • Securely delete your personal data using industry-standard deletion methods
  • Anonymise data where it's useful for statistical purposes but no longer needs to identify you
  • Retain data longer only if required by law or court order

If you close your account, non-essential data (such as profile photos and preferences) will be deleted within 30 days, while legally required records will be retained for the applicable statutory period.

6. International Data Transfers

We primarily store data within the United Kingdom and European Economic Area (EEA) on secure UK-based servers.

When International Transfers Occur:

Some of our service providers may transfer data outside the UK/EEA. When this happens, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place:

  • Google Analytics: May transfer data to the United States. Google is certified under the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework and we use Standard Contractual Clauses.
  • Cloud Storage Providers: We use UK and EEA-based data centers wherever possible. Any international transfers are protected by:
    • UK-approved Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
    • Adequacy decisions recognised by the ICO (for countries deemed to have adequate data protection laws)
    • Supplementary security measures including encryption and access controls

For detailed information about specific transfers, please contact us at support@flokjobs.com.

7. Your Rights

You have the following rights under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018:

  • Right of Access: Request a copy of your personal data and information about how we process it
  • Right to Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): Request deletion of your data, subject to legal obligations and retention requirements
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Request that we limit how we use your data while we consider a request or resolve a dispute
  • Right to Data Portability: Obtain your data in a machine-readable format (such as CSV or JSON) and request we transfer it to another service provider
  • Right to Object: Opt out of certain uses of your data, including:
    • Processing based on legitimate interests
    • Direct marketing (you can opt out at any time)
    • Automated decision-making
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent for processing where consent is the lawful basis (you can do this at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal)
  • Rights Relating to Automated Decision-Making: We do not currently use fully automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects. If this changes, we will update this policy and inform you of your rights to human review.

How to Exercise Your Rights:

Please contact us at:

  • Email: support@flokjobs.com
  • Post: Flok Jobs Ltd, 128 City Road, London, United Kingdom, EC1V 2NX

Response Time:

  • We will respond to your request within 30 calendar days
  • For complex requests, we may extend this by a further 60 days and will notify you with an explanation
  • We will verify your identity before processing requests to protect your data security
  • No Fee (Usually): Most requests are free. We may charge a reasonable fee if your request is clearly unfounded, repetitive, or excessive.

8. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Our Website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience, analyse usage, and deliver personalised content.

Types of Cookies We Use:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for basic website functionality (login, security, session management, platform operations). These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytical Cookies: Used to understand user behaviour and improve our services (e.g., Google Analytics). These help us see which pages are most popular and how users navigate our site.
  • Marketing Cookies: Used to deliver targeted advertising and track campaign effectiveness. These are only set with your consent.
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by external partners (Google Analytics, advertising networks) for analytics, advertising, and functionality.

Cookie Management:

You can control your cookie preferences in several ways:

  1. Cookie Consent Banner: When you first visit our site, you can choose which types of cookies to accept
  2. Cookie Settings: You can change your preferences at any time via the "Cookie Settings" link in our website footer
  3. Browser Settings: You can block or delete cookies through your browser settings, though this may affect website functionality

For detailed information about:

  • Specific cookies we use (names, purposes, durations)
  • How long each cookie persists
  • How to manage your cookie preferences
  • How to opt out of non-essential cookies

Please refer to our Cookie Policy (available at www.flokjobs.com/cookies)

Third-Party Analytics:

We use Google Analytics to understand how users interact with our platform. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect information such as:

  • How often users visit our site
  • Which pages they visit
  • Which features they use

This information is aggregated and anonymous. You can opt out of Google Analytics by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

9. Data Security

We take data security seriously and have implemented comprehensive technical and organisational measures to safeguard your personal data.

Technical Security Measures:

  • Encryption in Transit: All data transmitted between your device and our servers is encrypted using TLS/SSL with 256-bit encryption
  • Encryption at Rest: Sensitive data stored on our servers is encrypted using AES-256 encryption (bank details, identification documents, passwords)
  • Secure Infrastructure: We use industry-leading cloud providers with ISO 27001 certification and regular security audits
  • Firewalls & Intrusion Detection: Multi-layered firewalls and real-time intrusion detection systems monitor for suspicious activity
  • Regular Security Updates: We apply security patches and updates promptly to all systems and software
  • Secure Password Management: Passwords are hashed and salted using bcrypt or similar industry-standard algorithms - we never store passwords in plain text
  • Access Controls: Role-based access controls ensure staff can only access data necessary for their job function

Organisational Security Measures:

  • Limited Access: Only authorised personnel can access personal data, on a need-to-know basis
  • Data Access Logging: All access to personal data is logged and monitored for unusual activity
  • Employee Training: All staff receive regular data protection and security awareness training
  • Background Checks: Key personnel with access to sensitive data undergo background verification
  • Security Audits: We conduct regular internal security audits and engage third-party penetration testers annually
  • Incident Response Procedures: We have documented procedures for detecting, responding to, and reporting security incidents
  • Vendor Management: All third-party providers are assessed for security compliance before engagement

Your Responsibility:

Account Security: You are responsible for:

  • Keeping your password confidential and secure
  • Using a strong, unique password for your Flok Jobs account
  • Logging out after using shared or public devices
  • Notifying us immediately if you suspect unauthorized access to your account

Important Security Notice:

No method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we implement industry-standard security measures and continually work to improve our security posture, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

If you become aware of any security vulnerability or incident, please report it immediately to support@flokjobs.com.

10. Complaints & Data Protection

If you have concerns about how we handle your data, we want to hear from you.

Contact Us First:

  • Email: support@flokjobs.com
  • Post: Flok Jobs Ltd, 128 City Road, London, United Kingdom, EC1V 2NX

We will:

  • Acknowledge your complaint within 5 working days
  • Investigate thoroughly and respond within 30 calendar days
  • Provide a clear explanation of our findings and any actions taken

Escalation to the ICO:

If you're not satisfied with our response, or prefer to contact the regulator directly, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), the UK supervisory authority for data protection:

  • Website: www.ico.org.uk
  • Phone: 0303 123 1113
  • Post: Information Commissioner's Office, Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, Cheshire, SK9 5AF

The ICO provides guidance and can investigate complaints about data protection violations.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors.

How We Notify You:

Minor Changes:

  • Posted on our Website with a new "Last Updated" date
  • No additional notification required

Significant Changes:

  • Email notification sent to the email address associated with your account
  • Prominent notice displayed on our Website and platform
  • Reasonable notice period before changes take effect (typically 30 days)

What Constitutes a Significant Change:

  • Changes to the types of data we collect
  • New purposes for processing data
  • Changes to data sharing practices
  • Changes to your rights
  • Changes to retention periods
  • Changes to international transfers

Your Acceptance:

Your continued use of our services after the effective date of an updated Privacy Policy constitutes your acceptance of the changes. If you do not agree with the updated policy, you may close your account by contacting us at support@flokjobs.com.

Previous versions of this Privacy Policy are available upon request.
